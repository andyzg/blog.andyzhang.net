I'm lucky enough to say that I had more than a handful of interviews over the past few weeks. A few interviewers have viewed my resume as being unique and making me seem very passionate(which I am 200%) among the first year candidates and I wanted to share how I design my resume in order to convey this impression. For more details on this, feel free to check out my post below but if you'd like the gist of it, here it is:

Templates are a no no for me. You would just be another hay in a haystack, meaning they will forget about you. I like to design my own resume and make sure that I make use of all of its space without cramming it, all in 1 page. Why 1 page? This is because this shows that you are able to prioritize information. If you aren't able to prioritize for your resume, it can depict a lot about who you are in real life.

Content is also really important. If you are really passionate about software, this shouldn't be a problem for you. You should have projects all over your resume as a first year and talk about what they are and what you did.

I also like to add colours to mine because I find it makes my resume stand out. Most employers do notice these differences and it can also make your resume more fun to read.

Overall, it's really important to put a lot of work in your resume. I highly encourage future applicants to do the same and make sure that it looks nice, the content is good and that it gets reviewed.
